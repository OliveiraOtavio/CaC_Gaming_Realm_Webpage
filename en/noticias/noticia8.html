Third provincial meeting of videogames in Bahía Blanca

On April 22 and 23 at the Port of Bahía Blanca, the third provincial event of local videogames, organized by the Cultural Institute of the province of Buenos Aires, through the Undersecretary of Creative Industries and Cultural Innovation together with the Port Consortium. In this event, lasting 6 hours per day, more than 45,000 people of all ages participated who could enjoy countless proposals and activities.

References to the '80s and '90s were hand in hand with the classic arcades. Yes, the fichines inside of lookeado furniture that in our time had to be added blanks to add a life and see the action. “Insert Coin” and “Game Over” were the borders of our happiness. We played “Mortal Kombat”, “Wonder Boy” and "Snow Bross".

Later, chronologically, we went through game consoles like Nintendo, where "Mario Bross" was the star. But we were also able to see E-Sports tournaments, where soccer and basketball had their preferential place. The dispute was intense and became hot according to the juicy prizes at stake.

Beyond was the virtual reality sector. It was surprising to see so many young people flashing reality with the glasses that wrapped their visual field and led them to make accurate movements for the VR and comical for the caught off guard.

But it wasn't all play. The meeting gave a prominent place to the creative industry. because to achieve A final product of this type requires a multidisciplinary team. It's not for boys: programmers, designers, visual artists, musicians, broadcasters and a wide variety of specific professions according to the end goal.
